Article title: Amazon Leadership Principles
Date: Jan 12th, 2023
Source/Link: Amazon Leadership Principles

Summery of Article

This article is about the leadership principles that Amazon has incorporated and used at their company. The leadership principles are used on a daily basis and followed carefully. Amazon originally had 14 principles and added two more recently. These principles have helped improve the company's overall structure, productivity, and organization in the many different teams operating for Amazon. The 16 different principles that leaders follow at Amazon are, customer obsession, inventing and simplifying, learning and being curious, insisting on the highest standards, delivering results, striving to be earth's best employer, being humble and thinking ahead, taking ownership, being right a lot, hiring and developing the best, thinking big, having a bias for action, being frugal, earning trust, and diving deep. All of these principles help Amazon leaders to be the most effective and get the most done.

A third link between the article I chose and our course content involves Maslow's hierarchy of needs, specifically the fourth tier, "esteem". Amazon’s principle relates to and fits into this model of keeping people happy and motivated, by actively fulfilling the four tier in Maslow's hierarchy of needs. The principle is linked to Maslow’s hierarchy of needs because the company's principle allows leaders to fulfill a crucial tier in the hierarchy, which results in employees being happier and more productive.

How does the video relate to the course content?

This video relates to and covers concepts similar to those covered in the course. The first way that the video is related is demonstrated in the "linguistic" barriers section of the video. The video I chose explains that a communication barrier can be formed due to a linguistic barrier, meaning a language barrier. The course also explains the same concept with slightly different wording. Ultimately, the linguistic barrier shown in the video and the "process barrier" idea explained in the course are related, with both saying that language can cause a barrier between individuals. 

The second way the video is related to course content is when it explains "attitude" barriers. In this part of the video, the speaker explains what attitude barriers are and how they affect communication. Attitude barriers can be connected to the "process barriers" idea in the course. This is because the course explains that having the fear of being criticised leads to not implementing, which is a process barrier. The video says that social anxiety can be a psychological barrier. Fear of being criticised can cause a person to develop social anxiety. This shows that these two concepts are related but are demonstrated using different languages and are therefore connected. 

The third way the video is related to the course is when the video explains what perception barriers are. The auditor explains that perception barriers are formed when people do not talk clearly and simply. He says that they are formed because people interpret the message someone is saying differently. This is closely related to the semantic barrier idea in the course. They are related because they both touch on the point of there being a communication barrier when a message is not interpreted correctly.
